My husband and I went to Krave for brunch yesterday. It was good, but they're still working out the kinks. I'd say it's essentially a diner, just a notch above in terms of ingredients. We both ordered omelets- mine with home fries, his with hash browns. The omelets were both very tasty-- my mushroom omelet was made with fresh shitakes which I've never had before and which was very good. Neither of the omelets were quite properly cooked (his was a bit chewy, mine had a couple of over-jiggly bits). The home fries were average, and the hash-browns were pre-frozen patties. Portion size was tiny- I'd say the omelets were two eggs each and I got less than 1/4 cup of homefries. The toast was, sadly, cold and dry. The coffee was very good- not what I'd expect from diner coffee.
    Overall, everything was very tasty and any complaints we had were things I'd expect to get worked out when they've been open longer.

We stopped by Havana over the weekend and I chatted with the owner, Carlos.  He was very friendly and enthusiastic about their expansion.  They have just printed a take-out/delivery menu and will be delivering as soon as they find someone to do the deliveries.  They are still primarily sweets, but have a section of the menu for sandwiches and "snacks" - like croquettes and empanadas.  I was very happy to see that they have coxinhas, but they were out of them so I didn't get to try it.  I miss the coxinhas from Sabor Tropical.  They have some Brazilian items because both the head baker and Carlos' wife are Brazilian.

The coffee is a house blend of Brazilian, Colombian and Ethiopian beans - Carlos buys them separately and blends them himself.  I had a cortadito - Cuban espresso with a small splash of milk - and it was very good.  I'm looking forward to trying their other things.  I have more of a salt tooth rather than a sweet tooth, so I'm glad they serve things other than sweets, but everything looked good.

Havana Express
Cafe and Bakery
21-37 31st St.
718-545-9344
