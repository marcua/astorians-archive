There are so many options! 

Having recently moved here from Hell's Kitchen, that's the neighborhood that I'm most familiar with brunch-wise.  Eatery and HK, both on 9th Ave, are really popular bruch spots, and my favorites.  Both are cool/casual/new-Americanish, and would probably run you about $30 per person with an entree, coffee and a mimosa.  Both have outdoor seating, but on the sidewalk, not a garden. 

In terms of brunch places with outdoor garden seating, my favorite would probably be Home Restaurant in the West Village.  It has good food and a really cute outdoor garden area.  One drawback is that everytime I've been there, the service has been SLOWWWW.

Anyway, hope you are able to find a spot you like!

My friends and I have two favorites basically due to the relatively cheap menus and the free-flowing booze throughout the brunch. ;-)

Maracas: A totally campy, Mexican-themed restaurant. Brunch starts at 12pm on the weekends. It's $14.95 for the brunch menu which includes unlimited margaritas, bloody marys, or mimosas for 1.5 hours. (Additional alcohol charge after 1.5 hours)
http://www.maracasnyc.com/
33 Greenwich Ave btwn 6th and 7th

Essex Restaurant: Make a reservation! $16 includes three drinks, but they usually keep refilling your mimosas until you say stop. I think the food is better here than Maracas, but the atmosphere is totally different.
http://www.essexnyc.com
120 Essex Street

I went to Georgio's Country Grill, which is at 53rd Street and 9th Avenue, and was very impressed.  It's inexpensive (average brunch will run 8 to 15, excluding tax and tip) and food is very tasty. It's very busy because of tourists and the locals, but we only had to wait ten minutes for our table.  The people who work there are really friendly.

http://www.georgioscountrygrill.com/
