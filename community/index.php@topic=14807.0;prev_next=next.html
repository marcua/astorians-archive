It's kinda hard to do well. In particular, getting the texture and stickiness of the rice just right, with the right hint of vinegar, is not easy. Rice that's too sticky is really unpleasant next to fish, and if it's not starchy enough, it falls apart. Sushi apprentices in Japan spend 6 months doing nothing but making rice before they start being allowed to touch the fish. I certainly can't offer any real suggestions. If you're interested in learning to cook Japanese food, including some basic sushi, there's only one real resource: Tsuji's Japanese Cooking.
