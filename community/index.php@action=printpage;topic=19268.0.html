Title: It's your waterfront: Tell us what you think!
Post by: greenshoresnyc on September 05, 2010, 02:43:53 PM


We'd like to invite you  to join us at one or more neighborhood Listening Sessions. Each of these three sessions will focus on a different stretch of the Astoria-Long Island City waterfront. All are welcome! Light refreshments will be served.

   MONDAY, SEPTEMBER 13TH, 2010
Focus on the RAVENSWOOD WATERFRONT
This area includes the Big Allis power plant, Rainey Park, Costco Esplanade, Socrates Sculpture Park and Hallets Cove.
6:30-9PM
at VARIETY BOYS & GIRLS CLUB
21-02 30th Road in Astoria, Queens


   TUESDAY, SEPTEMBER 14TH, 2010
Focus on the HUNTERSPOINT WATERFRONT
   This area includes Newtown Creek, Hunters Point South, Gantry Plaza State Park, Queens West, Anable Basin & Anable Cove. 
6:30-9PM
at ST. MARY’S CHURCH BASEMENT
10-09 49th Avenue, Long Island City, Queens


   WEDNESDAY, SEPTEMBER 15TH, 2010
Focus on the ASTORIA PARK AREA
This area includes Pot Cove, Astoria Park, Ralph DeMarco Park, and the Northwest Corner of our Waterfront. 
6:30-9PM
at BOHEMIAN HALL
29-19 24th Avenue in Astoria, Queens

We'd appreciate it if you could help spread the word about this meeting to your members and mailing list. A pdf of a flyer is attached and you may also direct people to the Green Shores NYC website and Facebook event page:
http://www.facebook.com/?ref=logo#!/event.php?eid=151802951513396&ref=ts

Green Shores NYC is partnering with the Trust for Public Land to produce a community driven vision plan for the entire Astoria-Long Island City waterfront. We want to know: Where are you going along the waterfront? What activities are you doing there? Which areas are working well? Which areas aren’t working well?

Thank you,
