The White House

http://www.latimes.com/news/nationworld/world/la-fg-pullback15aug15,0,4840766.story?page=1&coll=la-home-center

Quote
Despite Bush's repeated statements that the report will reflect evaluations by Petraeus and Ryan Crocker, the U.S. ambassador to Iraq, administration officials said it would actually be written by the White House, with inputs from officials throughout the government.

That's right, kiddies.  After months and months of the President saying "Let's wait on the report by Petraeus," it turns out the report won't actually be written by him.  It will be written by the White House.  I know there are conservatives on this board.  And I would honestly like to know your opinions on this.  Do you think the report by the White House is to be trusted?  Don't you think it's odd that all these months the White House says that it won't run the war based on politicians recommendations, and would go by the advice of his commanders on the ground?  Yet the report won't actually be written by the commanders on the ground?

I try and keep an open mind with this, but how can we take this seriously?  How can we interpret this report as anything BUT political when it's being written by the White House, and NOT by the commanders on the ground?

Granted, the report hasn't been released yet, and won't be for a couple more weeks.  But I remain extremely skeptical.  Let's see how rosie a picture they paint.

Oh, did you hear about the car bomb that killed 250 people the other day?  One of the most catastrophic attacks in the entire war.
