This morning I cleaned some stray papers and whatever from my sidewalk before I went out for errands & shopping. I later found a pink violation ticket on my door, stating that there were 6 sheets of newspaper, a paper bag, and a glass bottle less that 18inches into the street in front of my house. The ticket is for $100?!  I did notice some papers between two parked cars, but they were further out in the street.

Here is a link to the Department of Sanitation page that contains links to the codes under which they issue violations and fines.  The first link on that page is a pdf of a compendium of various finable offences and what the fines may be.  At the beginning of page 5, it states that unless otherwise noted, all the laws listed apply to both residences and businesses.  Further down on page 5, it states that there is a program called "enforcement routing" relating to sidewalks and gutters, which sets out two one-hour periods of each day during which the city may issue fines to residents/residences for "dirty sidewalks and failure to clean 18 inches into the street."   The times are 8-9am, and noon-1pm.  The fine listed is $100-300, although it's a bit unclear whether that is for commercial properties only or includes residential properties. 

We got a Notice of Violation and Hearing last year for improper recycling for $25.  I fought it, arguing that the ticket itself was indecipherable and that the rules and regulations are confusing.  The violation was dismissed.  My recommendation is to send them a notice on or before the hearing date (if you were given one - if not, do it immediately) stating that you contest the fine and explain the reasons you are contesting it.  If you want, pm me and I can give you specific language you can use.

(If you look through all the things that are violations, it's almost comical how much of it is usually not enforced at all.  I'd say the rumour of a new quota isn't unreasonable.)
