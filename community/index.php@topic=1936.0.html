Re: astoria seems dirtier

Astoria is much more dirtier, and noisier recently, no doubt. It breaks my heart to see that it has taken a real step down in the past 12 months.

It doesn't help that so much of Astoria is Sunday night recycling, and with so many holidays on Monday, you often get several weeks of garbage piled up.

Some of my neighbors just don't see the problem, even when rats come out at night and eat the kitchen scaps that falls from their casually tossed bags they shrug or look at me with a blank face.

Not only is there a widening gap in public tastes for cleanliness, but the absentee landlords who don't live in their buildings add to this. Very few use a hose on a regular base to clean their stoops.

Many buildings that had 2 or 3 cans in front 10 years ago now have 10. Twenty years ago it was much much more cleaner, but we had a lot fewer people then. We are going to have lots of fun when 2/3 story buildings are soon to be replaced with 10/12 story buildings.

Can someone look up the statistics that the city publishes? Be interesting to see how Astoria rates with the city, and the rest of Queens.
