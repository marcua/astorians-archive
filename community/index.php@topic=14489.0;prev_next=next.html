If you are talking about the big field, then feel free, but be forwarned, many people there are extremely cautious (and I believe rightly so) about pits and pit mixes.  That being said, so long as your dog is friendly, and listens to you, and you are a responsible owner, then I don't think people will mind overall (although some people may keep a closer eye on your dog then others).  It is a public space, and so long as you obey the park rules (leash after 9 am / before 9 pm, clean up the poop, etc) then you are free to be there as well.  Some of the people also meet during the week both in the morning and in the evening.  The biggest issue people have is with aggressive dogs.  As an FYI (not to say you should choose this over the big field) for a while there was a group of friendly looking pits/mixes that were getting together near the triboro bridge to play together about the same time on the weekends, no idea if they are still there or not.

We used to bring our dogs there everyweekend, but realized we needed to get them to listen to us better on the come / leave it and other commands before we are really comfortable with them off leash.

Quote from: mcgowan on March 02, 2009, 01:03:13 PM
We used to bring our dogs there everyweekend, but realized we needed to get them to listen to us better on the come / leave it and other commands before we are really comfortable with them off leash.
Good for you. A well-trained dog is a well-behaved dog. It is a sign that the dog has a responsible owner. The COME and the LEAVE IT are fine. But for dealing with another aggressive dog, the STAY might also be useful to you. It is one of the hardest to teach, but so well worth the effort. Try this at home: command the dog to STAY, turn your back on the dog, fold your arms, and wait. If the dog STAYS, reward them immediately. If the dog walks around to face you, keeping your arms folded, not making eye contact, turn around 180 degrees. Do this as often as is necessary so the dog will understand that the reward only comes when he/she doesn't move.
